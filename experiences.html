  • Life in the US

There are some valuable experiences in my life. I lived with my parents, like most people did, and it is not very interesting life for any of us to talk about. I have been a novice monk when I was young, and I became a monk when I turned 21.

In my culture, boys should become a temporary monk at least once in a life time. Temporary monkhood takes about 5 days, 7 days, 10 days, or even more according to how long the person wants to be in the monkhood and how many days they have for their holiday.

I chose to be a monk for 5 days last winter break. It was a big experience because there are many rules that monks are supposed to follow. For example, monks are not supposed to have any solid food after 12PM and before about 5AM. It was tough for me to not have dinner for about first a few days, then I got used to with it.

Moreover, the robe that monks wear in my country has two pieces of rectangular cloth and a belt. One piece is for downwards from waist and one to cover up the whole body. The robe does not cover arms in casual and the cloth is not very thick either. It was a winter time when I was a monk. I beat the cold weather though.
