  • Life in the US

My experiences back home in my country are a lot. In my childhood, I go to school every day. It is not the interesting part because everybody did go to school when they were young.

The interesting part is I went to a very competitive school in town. The school is a small school that teaches from Grade 1 to Grade 9. We call those types of schools ‘Middle School’ because they offer classes up to Middle School Levels.

The school I went to had about 500 students in total. Despite being a small school, almost every student is trying very hard to get the best grades or perfect scores on the tests. Parents are also pushing their children to study a lot. I do not blame on parents making their children study, but children have to go to class at about 8 in the morning till about 3 PM. Then they have to go a private class what we call tuition class.

Tuition class is where teachers teach the students ahead of the school syllabus so that children will know ahead of school teaches them. After that class, children will spend about 3 hours studying on their own or with a private tutor. That sums up about twelve hours of school work for children each day. Only tuition classes and personal study time on weekends so there is only about five hours of school work on each day on weekends. However, I think that is too much for children at age around 10. I had been one of them so I think it is not fun at all for as a child.
